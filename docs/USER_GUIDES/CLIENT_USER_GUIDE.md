## Getting Started

### Creating an Account

1. Go to the platform homepage and click **Sign Up**.
2. Enter your first name, last name, email address, and a password.
3. Check your inbox for a confirmation email and click the link inside to verify your address.
4. Once verified, you are taken directly to your dashboard.

### Logging In

**Password login**
1. Click **Login** at the top of the page.
2. Enter your email and password, then click **Login**.

**Magic link (passwordless)**
1. Click **Login**, then choose **Email me a login link**.
2. Enter your email address and click **Send link**.
3. Check your inbox and click the link — you are logged in instantly. Links expire after a few minutes.

### Resetting Your Password

1. On the login page, click **Forgot password?**
2. Enter your email and click **Send reset link**.
3. Follow the link in the email and set a new password.

### Dashboard Overview

After login you land on your **Dashboard**, which shows:

- Active and recent orders at a glance
- Your wallet balance
- Loyalty points and current tier
- Unread notifications
- Quick links to place a new order or top up your wallet

### Getting Started Checklist

When you first create an account, a **Getting Started** checklist appears on your dashboard. It guides you through the recommended first steps:

1. Verify your email address
2. Complete your profile (name, timezone, communication preferences)
3. Read the Help Center introduction
4. Place your first order
5. Top up your wallet (optional — you can also pay by card at checkout)

The checklist closes automatically once all items are complete. You can also dismiss it manually if you prefer.

## Placing an Order

### Step 1 — Get a Price Estimate

Before creating an account or logging in, you can use the **Pricing Calculator** on the homepage.

1. Select the **service type** (e.g., Essay, Research Paper, Presentation).
2. Choose the **number of pages**, **deadline**, **academic level**, and **subject**.
3. An instant price estimate appears. Adjust any field to update the price in real time.
4. When satisfied, click **Proceed to Order** to open the full order form.

### Step 2 — Fill In the Order Form

The order form collects everything the writer needs:

| Field | What to enter |
|-------|--------------|
| Topic | The title or subject of the paper |
| Instructions | Detailed guidance — the more specific, the better |
| Paper type | Essay, research paper, case study, etc. |
| Academic level | High school, undergraduate, master's, PhD |
| Subject / discipline | Nursing, Business, Literature, etc. |
| Number of pages | Each page is approximately 275 words (double-spaced) |
| Deadline | How many hours from now you need the paper |
| Formatting style | APA, MLA, Chicago, Harvard, etc. |
| Spacing | Single or double |
| Writer level | Standard, Advanced, or Expert (price varies) |
| Additional files | Upload an outline, lecture notes, or rubric |

> **Tip:** Attaching a grading rubric is one of the most effective things you can do to improve quality.

### Step 3 — Apply a Coupon (Optional)

If you have a discount code, enter it in the **Coupon code** field before continuing. The discount is shown immediately. See [Discount & Coupon Codes](#discount--coupon-codes) for more detail.

### Step 4 — Review the Price Breakdown

Before reaching the payment screen, you will see an itemised price summary:

- Base price (pages × rate)
- Deadline surcharge (if applicable)
- Academic level adjustment
- Writer level adjustment
- Any discount applied
- **Total due**

The price is locked at this point — it will not change even if rates change before your paper is delivered.

### Step 5 — Pay and Confirm

Proceed to payment (see [Paying for Your Order](#paying-for-your-order)). Once payment is confirmed, your order is created and visible in **My Orders**.

## Paying for Your Order

You can pay with a **debit or credit card** or from your **wallet balance**.

### Pay with Card

1. On the payment screen, enter your card details.
2. Review the **payment disclosure** — it tells you who will appear on your bank statement. Read this before confirming.
3. Click **Pay now**. You may be asked by your bank to authenticate (e.g., 3D Secure / OTP).
4. On success, you receive an email receipt. The statement descriptor shown in the disclosure is what you will see on your bank statement.

### Pay from Wallet

If your wallet has sufficient balance:

1. On the payment screen, choose **Pay from wallet**.
2. Your balance is shown. Click **Confirm** to deduct the amount instantly.
3. No card entry or bank authentication needed.

### Payment Disclosure

Every client site is required by law to show who processes the payment and what will appear on your statement before and after each transaction. You will see this text on the payment screen and on your receipt. If you have questions about a charge on your statement, contact support with your order ID.

## Tracking Your Order

All your orders are listed under **My Orders**. Click any order to open its detail page.

### Order Statuses

| Status | What it means |
|--------|--------------|
| **Unpaid / Pending payment** | Payment has not been received yet |
| **Paid** | Payment confirmed; order is queued for assignment |
| **In progress** | A writer has been assigned and is working |
| **QA review / Under editing** | Work is being reviewed for quality; a download is available |
| **Submitted** | The writer has delivered; download is available |
| **Revision requested** | Your revision is being worked on |
| **Completed** | You have approved the order |
| **Disputed** | A dispute has been opened and is under review |
| **Cancelled** | The order was cancelled |
| **Refunded** | A refund has been processed |

### Order Detail Page

The detail page shows:

- Full order specifications
- Assigned writer (pen name only — contact details are never shared)
- Countdown to your deadline
- Files tab — for downloading delivered work and uploading your own files
- Messages tab — for communicating directly with the writer
- Timeline — a log of every status change

### Available Actions

The platform shows only the actions available at each stage. You will never see a button that does not apply.

| Action | When it appears |
|--------|----------------|
| **Cancel order** | Before a writer is assigned |
| **Approve order** | After work is submitted |
| **Request revision** | After work is submitted, within the revision window |
| **Raise dispute** | While the order is in progress, submitted, or completed |

3. Click **Download** next to the file you want.

> Files are available as long as your account is active. Download and save copies for your own records.

## Requesting a Revision

If the delivered work needs changes, you can request a free revision while the revision window is open.

1. Open the order.
2. Click **Request revision**.
3. Describe clearly what needs to change — be as specific as possible.
4. Submit. The writer is notified immediately and the order moves to **Revision requested**.

**Important:**
- The revision must be for changes within the original scope. A revision is not a new order.
- If the scope needs to expand (more pages, new sections), an adjustment charge may apply — support will contact you.
- The revision window closes after the order is approved or after a set period; check the order detail for the exact cut-off.

## Raising a Dispute

If you have a serious quality concern, missed deadline, or wrong file delivered, you can raise a dispute.

1. Open the order.
2. Click **Raise dispute** (visible when the order is in progress, submitted, or completed).
3. Select the reason:
   - Quality issue
   - Late delivery
   - Wrong file or format
   - Instructions not followed
   - Other
4. Add a summary explaining the problem.
5. Click **Submit**.

A support agent will review the dispute. You can see the status and any updates in the order detail page.

### Withdrawing a Dispute

If the issue is resolved before support closes it:

1. Open the order.
2. Click **Withdraw dispute**.

## Wallet & Payments

Your wallet is a pre-funded balance you can use to pay for orders instantly. It is faster than entering card details each time.

### Checking Your Balance

Your wallet balance is shown in the top navigation bar and on your dashboard.

### Topping Up

1. Go to **Wallet** in the navigation.
2. Click **Add funds**.
3. Enter the amount (minimum and maximum limits are shown on screen).
4. Enter your card details and confirm.
5. Funds appear in your balance immediately after the card charge succeeds.

### Transaction History

Under **Wallet → Transactions**, you can see every credit and debit:

- Top-ups (amount added, date)
- Order payments (order ID, amount deducted)
- Refunds credited to your wallet
- Loyalty point conversions (if enabled on your site)

### Refunds to Wallet

When a refund is issued and returned to your wallet, it appears as a credit in your transaction history and your balance updates immediately.

## Discount & Coupon Codes

### Entering a Code

Enter your discount code in the **Coupon code** field on the order form before proceeding to payment. The discount is applied instantly and shown in the price breakdown.

- If the code is valid, the discounted total and savings amount are displayed.
- If the code is invalid, expired, or not eligible for your order, a message explains why.

### Available Promotions

On the order form, there may be a **"View available promotions"** link showing codes you are already eligible for. The best available discount is sometimes applied automatically.

### Loyalty Tier Discounts

Clients on higher loyalty tiers (Silver, Gold, Platinum) may receive automatic discounts on every order without entering a code. Your tier discount is shown in the price breakdown.

## Loyalty Program & Rewards

Every completed paid order earns you loyalty points. Points accumulate and unlock higher tiers with better perks.

### Checking Your Points and Tier

Go to **Loyalty** in the navigation (or check your dashboard header) to see:

- Current points balance
- Current tier (Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum, or similar)
- Points needed to reach the next tier
- Lifetime points earned

### Tiers

| Tier | How to qualify | Example benefits |
|------|---------------|-----------------|
| Bronze | Default — all clients start here | Standard pricing |
| Silver | Reach the Silver threshold | Tier discount on orders |
| Gold | Reach the Gold threshold | Higher discount + priority support |
| Platinum | Reach the Platinum threshold | Maximum discount + exclusive perks |

Exact thresholds and discounts are set by each site — check the **Loyalty** page for your site's values.

### Badges and Milestones

As you reach spending or order milestones (e.g., first 10 orders, first $100 spent), the platform awards you badges. Badges are shown on your profile and dashboard.

### Redeeming Points

1. Go to **Loyalty → Rewards**.
2. Browse the available rewards (discount codes, wallet credit, service vouchers, etc.).
3. Click **Redeem** on a reward you want.
4. Confirm the redemption. Points are deducted immediately and the reward is issued.

**Redemption types include:**
- Discount code — applied automatically to your next order
- Wallet credit — added to your balance immediately
- Voucher code — provided for external use

Redemption requests may occasionally require a short approval step; you will be notified when the reward is fulfilled.

## Messaging Your Writer

Every order has a **Messages** tab where you can communicate directly with your assigned writer.

1. Open the order and click **Messages**.
2. Type your message and press **Send**.
3. You can attach files to messages.
4. The writer is notified in real time and typically responds within a few hours.

**Best practices:**
- Send any additional instructions or clarifications as soon as possible after placing the order — earlier is always better.
- Be specific. "Please use more academic language in section 2" is more useful than "improve the writing."
- Do not share personal contact information (email, phone, social media). All communication should stay within the platform.

## Troubleshooting

### I did not receive a confirmation email

- Check your spam or junk folder.
- Add the site's support email to your contacts and try registering again.
- Contact support if the problem persists.

### My payment failed

- Check that the card details are correct and the card is not expired.
- Ensure your bank has not blocked the transaction (some banks block online writing services by default). Call your bank if needed.
- Try a different card, or top up your wallet first and pay from there.

### The login link expired

Magic links expire quickly for security. Click **Email me a login link** again to get a fresh one.

### I cannot see my order

- Make sure you are logged in with the same email address used when placing the order.
- Check the **All orders** filter — it may be filtered to active orders only.
- Contact support with your order reference number.

### The download link is not working

- Check your browser's downloads folder — the file may have downloaded without a prompt.
- Try a different browser.
- Contact support with your order ID.

### I want to cancel an order that has already been assigned

Orders can be cancelled without charge before a writer is assigned. Once a writer is working, cancellation is handled on a case-by-case basis through the dispute or support process. Contact support as soon as possible.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I request a specific writer?**  
A: Yes. If you have worked with a writer before and know their pen name or ID, add them as a **preferred writer** on your profile. When you place a new order, the system first invites your preferred writer. If they are unavailable, the order opens to the wider writer pool.

**Q: What happens if my paper is delivered late?**  
A: A late delivery gives you grounds to raise a dispute. Support will review the timeline and may issue a partial refund or credit, depending on the circumstances.

**Q: Is my personal information shared with the writer?**  
A: No. Writers only see your order instructions, uploaded files, and messages. Your name, email, payment details, and other personal information are never shared.

**Q: What is the difference between a revision and a new order?**  
A: A revision is a free correction of work already delivered, within the original scope. If you need significantly more content (additional pages, new sections not in the original brief), that requires a new order or an adjustment to the existing one.

**Q: Can I get a refund?**  
A: Refund eligibility depends on the order status and the reason. The Refund Policy in the footer explains the conditions in full. Refunds are returned either to your original payment method or to your wallet. Contact support to start a refund request.

**Q: How do I know who processes my payment?**  
A: This is shown in the payment disclosure on every payment screen and on your receipt. The company name shown there will appear on your bank statement.

**Q: Will the writer know how much I paid?**  
A: No. Writers see only their own compensation rate, not the total price you paid.

**Q: What file formats are accepted for uploads?**  
A: Common formats are accepted — Word (.doc, .docx), PDF, plain text (.txt), and images (.jpg, .png). If you need to upload a different format, contact support.

**Q: My loyalty points did not appear after an order was completed.**  
A: Points are credited when the order status changes to **Completed** (after you approve the work). If points are still missing 24 hours after approval, contact support with your order ID.
